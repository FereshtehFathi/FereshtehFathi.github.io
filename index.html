02 — Research

Selected work

Undergraduate Thesis — Altered Functional Connectivity in Bipolar Disorder
fMRI

Investigated task-based functional connectivity in individuals with bipolar disorder versus healthy controls, using the open UCLA CNP dataset. Thesis focused on the Task-Switching paradigm; currently extending the analysis to the Balloon Analog Risk Task and Stop Signal Task as part of a larger collaborative study. Designed the full preprocessing pipeline and ran ROI-to-ROI and seed-to-voxel connectivity analyses. Manuscript in preparation. Supervised by Dr. Qazal Taghikhani.

CONN toolbox · SPM12 · UCLA CNP dataset
Tracking Semantic Representations Across the Visual Hierarchy
Neuromatch Academy · RSA

Applied representational similarity analysis to fMRI data (Kay et al., 2008) to examine how semantic similarity is encoded across the visual hierarchy (V1–V4, V3A/B, LatOcc). Developed a weighted hierarchical semantic similarity matrix, computed Pearson-based neural similarity matrices per ROI, and correlated them with semantic structure using Spearman rank correlations. Implemented the full analysis pipeline in Python; contributed to hypothesis formulation, visualization, and the group presentation.

Python · RSA · Spearman correlation

Fathiziarani, F. et al. (in preparation). Altered Functional Connectivity Across Cognitive Tasks in Bipolar Disorder: An Open fMRI Dataset Study.
